AI Literacy Capstone — Workforce Trends, APAC Focus

Adoption raced ahead. Trust didn’t keep up.

PwC’s Global Workforce Hopes & Fears Survey 2025 and BCG’s AI at Work series (APAC 2025 + Global 2026) were fielded independently — different teams, different years. Read side by side, they tell one story: the technology gap closed faster than the trust gap, and trust is now the main constraint on value. Start below by checking where your own org sits.

Sources & methodology

Two independently fielded surveys, read together for thematic convergence — not statistically merged.

PwC Global Workforce Hopes and Fears Survey 2025 — ~49,800 respondents, 48 countries, 28 sectors, fielded Jul–Aug 2025. Global report · Singapore edition (n=1,051).

BCG AI at Work — Is Asia Pacific Leading the Way? (APAC edition, October 2025) — n=4,559 across Australia, China, India, Indonesia, Japan, Malaysia, Singapore, South Korea, Thailand.

BCG AI at Work — Strategy Matters More Than Tools (Global, Fourth Edition, June 2026) — n=11,749 across 14 markets.

Figures are quoted as published. Where a chart value was read visually from a source PDF rather than stated in body text, it is marked with “~”. The benchmark values used in the diagnostic dials above are mapped onto a common 0–100 scale by theme, since the two surveys use different question wording. Gaps are flagged at 10 percentage points or more against the selected market’s own average (Singapore or India); the BCG “high-performing” (Reshape/Invent) benchmark is shown only as a faint reference tick on each dial, not as the basis for flagging. This mapping, the 10-point threshold, and the behaviour/COE recommendations are an editorial framework built for this dashboard, not a statistical finding from either report. Treat the diagnostic as a starting structure for a real internal pulse survey, not a substitute for one. This dashboard was built as a hands-on AI-literacy exercise: extracting findings from four source documents, identifying cross-report themes, and translating them into a leadership-behaviour and regional-activation framework using Claude.
